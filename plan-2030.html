National Agricultural Roadmap 2026-2030

Transforming Subsistence to Commercial

A comprehensive plan to build a climate-resilient, technology-driven agricultural system in Bangladesh[cite: 1, 2]. Our focus is mitigating arable land loss while enhancing productivity through stress-tolerant varieties and diversification[cite: 3].

Strategic Pillars

Core Management Strategies

Climate-Smart Agriculture

Widespread adoption of flood, drought, and saline-tolerant varieties. Expanding small-scale irrigation and reducing waterlogging in coastal areas[cite: 7]. Promoting DSR and AWD techniques to save water and reduce methane emissions[cite: 8].

Precision & Tech

Scaling mechanization for sowing and harvesting to reduce labor costs[cite: 13, 14]. Utilizing ICT apps like "CropClinic" for real-time pest diagnosis and fertilizer recommendations[cite: 15]. Adopting mid-tech greenhouse models for export-ready produce[cite: 16].

Input Management

Site-specific fertilizer optimization (FRG-2018) and organic fertilizer promotion[cite: 17, 18]. Strengthening Integrated Pest Management (IPM) through pheromone traps and biological controls[cite: 19].

Nutrition & Security

Diversification & Infrastructure

Moving beyond rice to high-value crops like maize, pulses, oilseeds, fruits, and spices to improve farmer income and nutrition[cite: 9, 10]. We are driving commercialization through agro-processing of potatoes, mangoes, and jackfruits[cite: 11].

Storage & Logistics

Developing multi-purpose storage for onions and potatoes to reduce post-harvest losses[cite: 23].

Extension & Training

The DAE aims to train 20% of farmers in sustainable, modern techniques[cite: 21, 22].

Land Use Protection

Implementing policies to prevent the annual 1% loss of agricultural land to non-agricultural purposes[cite: 24].
